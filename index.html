Navigating Work-Life Balance

Adults (18+) Level: A2-B2 60 Mins

Learning Objectives

  • Use vocabulary related to stress, relaxation, and routines.
  • Answer professional and personal questions fluently.
  • Express and defend opinions about modern work culture.

1. Warm-Up Discussion

1. How do you relax after a long day of work or study? 🛋️

Example: "I usually relax by listening to a podcast or cooking dinner while drinking a glass of water."

2. Are you a morning person or a night owl? 🦉

Example: "I am definitely a morning person. I feel most productive right after I wake up and have coffee."

3. Do you prefer working from home or in an office? 💻

Example: "I prefer a hybrid model. I like working from home for focus, but the office is better for meetings."

4. What is your favorite day of the week and why? 📅

Example: "Friday is my favorite because the weekend is starting, and the mood in the office is very relaxed."

5. How much coffee or tea do you drink every day to stay awake? ☕

Example: "I try to limit myself to two cups of coffee a day, otherwise I can't sleep at night."

4. Workplace Role-Play

Scenario 1: Asking for Time Off

You have been feeling stressed and overworked lately. You need to ask your manager for a few days off to recharge.

Role A: Employee

  • Explain that you are feeling close to burnout.
  • Request specific dates for your time off.
  • Phrase: "I'd like to request some time off next week because..."

Role B: Manager

  • Express concern for their well-being.
  • Ask how their current projects will be handled.
  • Phrase: "I understand. Who will cover your urgent tasks while you're away?"

Scenario 2: Setting Boundaries

A colleague keeps sending you work emails late at night and expects immediate replies. Discuss this issue with them.

Role A: You

  • Acknowledge their hard work, but state your boundaries.
  • Politely say you unplug after 7 PM.
  • Phrase: "I noticed you sent emails late last night. I usually disconnect after 7 PM to..."

Role B: Colleague

  • Explain that you are a 'night owl' and prefer working late.
  • Agree to a compromise (e.g., scheduling emails).
  • Phrase: "I'm sorry, I just work better at night. I didn't mean to pressure you."
