📖 2. Key Vocabulary

Click on a word card to reveal its definition and see it used in context.

Addicted (adj)
Unable to stop doing something as a habit.
"Many teenagers are completely addicted to scrolling through TikTok."
Convenient (adj)
Fitting in well with a person's needs, activities, or plans.
"Online shopping is very convenient because you don't have to leave the house."
Algorithm (noun)
A set of mathematical rules used by computers to calculate or solve problems.
"The YouTube algorithm knows exactly what kind of videos I like to watch."
Privacy (noun)
The state of being free from public attention or being observed.
"You should be careful about your digital privacy and not share passwords."
Go viral (verb phrase)
To quickly become extremely popular on the internet.
"His funny video with the cat went viral and got millions of views."
Disconnect (verb)
To detach or separate; to stop using technology temporarily.
"When I go camping in the mountains, I completely disconnect from the internet."
Virtual (adj)
Existing or occurring on computers or on the internet.
"During the pandemic, all our team meetings became virtual."
Innovative (adj)
Featuring new methods; advanced and original.
"Apple is known for creating highly innovative tech products."

🗣️ 3. Main Speaking Practice

Answer the questions below. Utilize the buttons for brainstorming ideas or to view sample structures.

Easy 1. What are the most common ways you use your smartphone?
Idea Prompts:
• Communication (texting, calling).
• Entertainment (music, videos, games).
• Utilities (maps, banking, taking photos).
Short Answer:
"I mainly use my phone for chatting with friends and listening to Spotify."
Extended Answer:
"I use my smartphone for almost everything. Aside from basic communication like texting and calling, I use it heavily for navigation when driving, and for mobile banking. It is incredibly convenient."
Easy 2. Which social media platforms do you use the most, and why?
Idea Prompts:
• Facebook / Instagram for friends and photos.
• LinkedIn for professional networking.
• TikTok / YouTube for entertainment.
Short Answer:
"I use Instagram the most because I like looking at photos and short videos."
Extended Answer:
"My favorite platform is probably YouTube because I can learn new skills from tutorials. I also use Facebook daily, mainly to keep in touch with older family members who live far away."
Easy 3. How often do you upgrade or buy a new phone?
Idea Prompts:
• Every year (to have the newest tech).
• Every 3-4 years (when the old one breaks).
• I don't care about new phones, they are too expensive.
Short Answer:
"I usually buy a new phone every three years when my battery gets weak."
Extended Answer:
"I am not someone who needs the latest gadget. I usually wait until my current phone completely breaks down or becomes too slow to use, which is usually around three to four years."
Medium 4. What are some negative effects of spending too much time on social media?
Idea Prompts:
• Wasting time / Procrastination.
• Comparing your life to others (feeling jealous or sad).
• Eye strain and lack of physical exercise.
Short Answer:
"It makes people waste a lot of time and sometimes feel bad about their own lives."
Extended Answer:
"One major negative effect is that people become addicted and waste hours scrolling. Furthermore, social media often shows a 'perfect' fake life, which causes people to constantly compare themselves and feel insecure."
Medium 5. How has technology changed the way we work in the last 10 years?
Idea Prompts:
• Remote work / Zoom meetings.
• Cloud storage and sharing documents instantly.
• The boundary between work and home is disappearing.
Short Answer:
"It has allowed many people to work from home using virtual meetings."
Extended Answer:
"Technology has completely revolutionized the workplace. The biggest change is the ability to work remotely. Tools like Zoom and Google Workspace allow teams to collaborate instantly from different countries, which wasn't possible before."
Opinion 8. Do you think AI (Artificial Intelligence) is a threat to human jobs?
Idea Prompts:
• Yes: AI can do repetitive tasks faster and cheaper than humans.
• No: AI will create new types of jobs and just be a helpful tool.
• It depends on the industry (e.g., tech vs. physical labor).
Short Answer:
"Yes, I think AI will replace many basic office jobs in the future."
Extended Answer:
"I believe AI is a double-edged sword. It will definitely eliminate certain repetitive jobs, like basic data entry or customer service. However, it will also create new opportunities. We need to adapt and learn how to use AI as a tool rather than fear it."
Opinion 9. "Social media makes people more lonely." Do you agree or disagree?
Idea Prompts:
• Agree: People talk to screens instead of real people face-to-face.
• Disagree: It helps us connect with friends who live far away.
• Virtual friends are not the same as real-life friends.
Short Answer:
"I agree. People spend too much time looking at phones instead of talking."
Extended Answer:
"I strongly agree with this statement. Even though we have hundreds of 'friends' online, those connections are often superficial. People spend less time meeting face-to-face, which leads to a deep sense of isolation and loneliness in the real world."
Opinion 10. Should the government regulate or restrict how tech companies use our private data?
Idea Prompts:
• Yes, companies sell our data without our permission.
• Yes, to protect individuals from hackers and scams.
• No, targeted ads are actually useful and free platforms need to make money.
Short Answer:
"Yes, they should. Our privacy is very important and companies shouldn't sell it."
Extended Answer:
"Absolutely. Currently, large tech companies have too much power and collect massive amounts of personal data without clear transparency. Governments must implement strict laws to protect user privacy and prevent data from being exploited for profit."

🎭 4. Professional Role-Play

Work with a partner. Choose a scenario and practice your conversation skills. Expand the dialogue hints if you get stuck.

Scenario A: The "Digital Detox"

Situation: Student A is addicted to their smartphone and social media. Student B is a concerned friend suggesting a weekend trip with no screens.

Role A: Make excuses. Say you need your phone for "emergencies" and to check emails.

Role B: Persuade them. Explain the benefits of disconnecting and enjoying nature.

B: "Hey, you've been staring at your screen all day. We should go camping this weekend and leave our phones at home. A digital detox!"

A: "Leave my phone? I can't do that. What if there is an emergency?"

B: "We will have one emergency phone in the car. It will be so relaxing to disconnect from the internet."

A: "But I need to check my work emails on Saturday morning..."

Scenario B: Tech Support

Situation: Student A is an older relative who is very confused about a new app (like Zoom or Mobile Banking). Student B is helping them.

Role A: Express frustration. Say the buttons are too confusing and you are afraid of breaking it.

Role B: Be patient. Explain step-by-step how to use it in very simple terms.

A: "I don't understand this banking app. There are too many buttons. I am afraid I will lose my money!"

B: "Don't worry, it is very secure. Let's do it together. First, just tap the blue icon on your screen."

A: "Okay, I tapped it. Now it asks for a password?"

B: "Yes. Just type the password we created yesterday, then press the 'Log In' button."
