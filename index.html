📚 2. Core Vocabulary

Review these 8 essential B2 travel terms. Click "Show Example" to see how they are used in context.

1. Itinerary (n.)

📋

A detailed plan or schedule of a journey, including the route and places to visit.

"We have a very packed itinerary for tomorrow, starting with a museum tour at 8 AM."

2. Tourist trap (n.)

🪤

A place that attracts many tourists and charges unusually high prices.

"Avoid the restaurants near the main square; they are massive tourist traps."

3. Off the beaten path

🧭

A phrase meaning to go to a place that is not well known or popular with tourists.

"We rented a car so we could explore villages off the beaten path."

4. Ecotourism (n.)

🍃

Tourism directed toward natural environments, intended to support conservation efforts.

"Costa Rica is famous for its ecotourism, offering incredible wildlife experiences."

5. Immerse (v.)

🌊

To involve yourself completely in a culture, activity, or environment.

"To learn the language faster, you should immerse yourself in the local culture."

6. Culture shock (n.)

😲

The feeling of confusion and anxiety experienced when visiting a completely different society.

"Moving to Tokyo from a small town gave me massive culture shock at first."

7. Jet lag (n.)

🥱

Extreme tiredness felt after a long flight across different time zones.

"I slept all day yesterday because I was suffering from terrible jet lag."

8. Breathtaking (adj.)

✨

Astonishingly beautiful or magnificent; taking your breath away.

"The view from the top of the mountain was absolutely breathtaking."

🎭 4. Role-Play

Practice these real-life scenarios with a partner, or practice both roles yourself to build confidence and fluency.

🏨

Scenario 1: Hotel Problems

The Situation: You have just arrived at your hotel after a long, exhausting flight. However, the receptionist says they cannot find your reservation in the system.

Role A: The Guest

You are tired and suffering from jet lag. Politely but firmly explain that you booked online and show your confirmation email.

Role B: The Receptionist

Apologize for the inconvenience. Ask for their booking reference number and offer them a free drink while they wait.

Guest: "Excuse me, I'm checking in under the name Smith. I'm quite exhausted from my flight."

Receptionist: "Let me check... I'm so sorry, Mr. Smith, I can't seem to find your reservation in our system."

Guest: "That's strange. I booked it two months ago. Here is my confirmation email on my phone."

Receptionist: "Thank you. Could you read me the booking reference number? Please have a seat and a complimentary coffee while I sort this out."

🗺️

Scenario 2: Planning the Itinerary

The Situation: You and your friend are planning a trip to a new country. You have very different travel styles and need to reach a compromise.

Role A: The Adventurer

You want to go off the beaten path, try extreme sports, and completely immerse yourself in nature.

Role B: The Relaxer

You want a stress-free itinerary, nice restaurants, and to avoid tourist traps. You just want to relax.

Adventurer: "I was thinking we could rent bikes and cycle through the mountains for the first three days."

Relaxer: "To be honest, that sounds a bit exhausting. I was hoping we could just find a nice resort and relax by the beach."

Adventurer: "But we should immerse ourselves in the local culture! Resorts are just tourist traps."

Relaxer: "How about a compromise? We can do your adventure trip for two days, but then we spend the weekend relaxing."
